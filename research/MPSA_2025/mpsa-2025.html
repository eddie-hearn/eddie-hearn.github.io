Motivation

  • Global shocks tend to lead to trade disruption beyond market oriented factors (Eichengreen and O’Rourke, 2009; Eichengreen and Irwin, 2010; Hayakawa and Mukunoki, 2021)
  • International institutions such as RTAs have been shown to promote trade in general (Baier and Bergstrand, 2007)
  • Do international institutions constrain state behavior during shocks? (Baccini and Kim, 2012; Nicita and Tresa, 2023)

Theory

  • The design of international institutions is not homogeneous and influences effectiveness (Koremenos et al., 2001)
  • Legal enforceability of provisions is likely to determine the binding power of trade institutions
  • Increased cost of cheating, lack of flexibility, or the long shadow of the future may have perverse effects on compliance and bargaining (Rosendorff and Milner, 2001)

PTA Design (Hofmann et al., 2017)

  • “The Parties recognize the importance of regional and multilateral initiatives to eliminate bribery and corruption in international trade and investment. The Parties shall work jointly to encourage and support appropriate initiatives in relevant international fora. (US – Panama, Article 18.9 on cooperation in international fora)

PTA Design (Hofmann et al., 2017)

  • “ Neither Party may maintain or institute any duties, taxes or other fees and charges imposed on, or in connection with, the exportation of goods to the other Party, or any internal taxes, fees and charges on goods exported to the other Party that are in excess of those imposed on like goods destined for internal sale .” (EU – Republic of Korea, Article 2.11, Section C on Non‐Tariff Measures

Hypotheses

  • Legal enforceability of RTAs has a nonlinear and conditional effect on trade
  • In stable economic times, legal enforceability signals a credible commitment but high levels hinder flexibility (non-linear)
  • During a global shock, high levels of legal enforceability provides resilience (reversal)

Conclusions

  • The relationship between legal enforceability and bilateral trade is found to be non-linear
  • During crises, legal enforceability is associated with stable trade
  • Trade agreements that strike a balance between enforceability and flexibility may be optimal
