Will this increased exposure create backlash to globalization?

  • Material: competition over finite resources between groups (Scheve and Slaughter 2001; Mayda 2006; Mansfield and Mutz 2009)
  • Symbolic: replacement of cultural values, norms, and identities (Citrin, Reingold, Walters, & Green, 1990)

Individual Cultural Threat (Newman, Hartman, and Taber 2012)

  • Acculturation theory: displacement of original cultural patterns leading to cultural change
  • Sociocultural adaptation: individual ability to thrive in new sociocultural environment (complete daily tasks, make friends, communicate)
  • Sociocultural competencies: tools that allow for positive sociocultural interaction (Differnt environments requires different sets of competency)

Experimental Design

  • Japanese respondents are exposed to foreign language in a natural setting (requires sociocultural adaptation)
  • Negative adaptation among individuals lacking appropriate "global" sociocultural competencies produces an individual threat (sociocultural competencies do not match environment)
  • Reduced sociocultural competence leads to decreased support for globalization

Sociocultural Competence

  • Age is strongly correlated with globalization attitudes in Japan
  • Age may provide a strong indicator of individual sets of sociocultural competencies
  • The impact of foreign exposure is considered by age

Treatment

  • Participants are randomly assigned to 1 of 3 experimental groups (Vietnamese, control, English)
  • The treatment consists of a survey question appearing in either Vietnamese or English (The control group receives question in Japanese)
  • Following the treatment, respondents receive a general measure of globalization attitudes and a measure of immigration attitudes (Japanese)

English Treatment

Next, we would like to ask some questions about international affairs.

Globalization refers to the increased integration of people, companies and governments around the world. While globalization is not a new phenomenon, in the last 30 years there has been a rapid expansion in the interconnectivity of the world’s economies, politics, and cultures.

Which of the following statements best matches your opinion about the effect of globalization on Japan in general.

          1. Globalization has mostly benefited Japan.
         2. While there are both positive and negative aspects, globalization has slightly benefited Japan.
         3. Globalization has neither benefited nor harmed Japan.
         4. While there are both positive and negative aspects, globalization has slightly harmed Japan.
         5. Globalization has mostly harmed Japan.

Conclusions

  • Treatment relatively weak
  • Current immigration policies isolate foreign workers from native populations
  • Reduced emphasis on assimilation of foreign residents could increase individual-level cultural threat
